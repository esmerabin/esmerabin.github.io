About

Esme Rabin is a consulting archivist available for work with institutions, organizations, private individuals, and families. After earning her Master's in Library & Information Science with a concentration in Archives from Simmons University in 2016, she has worked mainly on personal family collections. As a consultant, she works with clients to organize, process, and/or digitize a wide variety of materials, including photographs, documents, books, and other print media. She recognizes that each client has unique materials and needs, and works closely with the client on an individual basis to ensure their collections are preserved thoroughly and in keeping with industry standards.

Services

First Steps

To begin, we meet with clients to determine the size of the collection, the types of materials included, the physical state of the items, and the needs of the client. In addition to an initial meeting, this gives us an opportunity to look at the materials themselves. Once we have the relevant information, we can then begin to develop a project plan.

Survey & Inventory

In this stage of a project, we go through the collection in greater detail. This allows us to assess the physical state of items and come to a more complete understanding of what content actually makes up the collection. We will compile thorough inventories that cover content, location, and physical state of items. This step helps inform preservation decisions moving forward.

Preservation

Steps to preserve items within a collection are different for different materials. We work to preserve both physical and digital materials in keeping with industry standards, and offer recommendations for our clients moving forward. This step may include slating certain objects for digitization, placing items in acid-free storage, identifying a storage space with a fairly controlled climate, making multiple backups of digital files, and more.

Digitization

For physical materials, we offer high-quality digitization services. Working with clients on an individual basis, we identify the extent of the collection to be digitized. For some clients, this may be the entire collection; for others, it may only be a select few items. Once digitized, these materials are cataloged and preserved in keeping with industry best practices, and provided to the client in an easily accessible format.

Transcription

In the case of documents with writing or text, whether digitized or not, we can provide typed transciptions. Documents needing transcription may range from official forms to handwritten letters and notes.

Genealogical Research

If desired for a family collection, we offer genealogy services. These may include developing a family tree, conducting genealogical research to fill in missing names, and locating documents relating to family members in order to provide a fuller picture of their lives. The results of this genealogical research can be provided in any format preferred by the client.
